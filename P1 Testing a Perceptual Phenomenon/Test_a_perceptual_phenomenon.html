Background Information

In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participants task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition. Questions For Investigation

1. What is our independent variable? What is our dependent variable?¶

Independent variable is was whether the word name and font color were the same (congruent words) or different (incongruent words).

Dependent variable is the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists of congruent and incongruent words.

2. What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.¶

The data has two set of samples and we do not know population parameters such as mean and variance. Therefore, t-test is the best statistical test assuming the data was random sampled and normal distributed. Since our participants were measured at two time points we use paired t-test for two related samples.

We could use two tail paired t-test for this task. However, given the information that in the incougruent words conditions the participants had to say out loud the color of the words that doesn't match its ink color. It seems harder job. My test results were: 10.39s and 20.85s for congruent and incongruent, respectively. So, we use one tail paired t-test for this task with the following hypotheses:

Ho = The mean times to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists of words are the same at an alpha of .05.

Ha = The mean time to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists of congruent words are less than incongruent words at an alpha of .05.

6. Optional: What do you think is responsible for the effects observed? Can you think of an alternative or similar task that would result in a similar effect? Some research about the problem will be helpful for thinking about these two questions!¶

When the participants only had to read the words which names match the colors, their brains only need to work on reading the word and say out loud. Meanwhile, when they had to read the words which names does not match the colors, they have to say out loud the color without be interfered with the meaning of the words. The second task leads to more mistakes and longer time to achieve.

Another interesting example about how our brain works is a game call "Follow my instructions". Test person tells participants to do something like "Put your hand on your shoulder!" but the test person put her hand on her head. Many people just see it and do the same as the test person while they are supposed to do what she says. However, there are few people who focus on what she says will do much better. Our brain works quicker with automate reading and automate seeing but slower with interpretating their meaning to us. In order to make our brain works effectively, we need to filter distractions and pay attention to our goals.
